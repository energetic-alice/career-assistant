# UK IT Job Market Overview as of April 2026

The UK IT job market in 2026 shows steady growth driven by digital transformation, AI adoption, and rising IT spending, with a persistent skills shortage in specialist areas like cybersecurity, data engineering, AI/ML, and DevOps, while generalist roles remain flat.[1][3][4] Overall vacancies are stabilizing at around 734,000 total UK jobs (Q4 2025 data, up +10,000), with tech hubs in London (59% of sector value), Manchester, Leeds, and Birmingham leading hiring; base salaries grew ~7.5% YoY amid skills-first hiring.[2][3][5] Demand favors hybrid/remote roles, with high growth in AI-related postings (3x average) and cybersecurity shortages (71% of companies).[3][4]

Detailed data per role is limited in available sources, which aggregate trends rather than provide granular vacancy counts or competition ratios from specified job boards (e.g., itjobswatch.co.uk, reed.co.uk, totaljobs.com, cwjobs.co.uk) or levels.fyi. Specific reports like Stack Overflow 2025/2026, LinkedIn Workforce Report, Hired, Dice, Ravio, Glassdoor, GitHub Octoverse, or Hays were not found in results. Below are tables summarizing verifiable data by role category; "data not available" noted where specifics absent.
